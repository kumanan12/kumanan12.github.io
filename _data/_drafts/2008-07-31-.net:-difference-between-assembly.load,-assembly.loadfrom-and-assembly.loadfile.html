I was curious to know about the difference between


  1. Assembly.load

  2. Assembly.loadfrom

  3. Assembly.loadfile

I could not get easy answers by googling. Here is my explanation in simple terms. For more detail information, please look at the references at the bottom of this post.


1. Assembly.load


When you do call Assemly.load by just giving the name like Assembly.load("myassembly.dll");


the CLR will try to locate it in the directory where the application is running. if it is not available, it will throw a FileNotFoundException.


Example:


Case 1: Loading the assembly by referring the name.



Assembly assembly = Assembly.Load("Syncfusion.Shared.Web");

I got this exception

System.IO.FileNotFoundException: Could not load file or assembly 'Syncfusion.Shared.Web' or one of its dependencies. The system cannot find the file specified.


After I copied this assembly into my bin\debug directory, It worked.

If you running in the release version, you have to put the in the bin\release folder.


Case 2: Loading the assembly by referring the full name.


Assembly.Load("System.ServiceProcess, Version=2.0.0.0, Culture=neutral, PublicKeyToken=b03f5f7f11d50a3a");


When you load an assembly like this, It will look for this assembly in the GAC.
